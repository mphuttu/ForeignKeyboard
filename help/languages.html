Polytonic (Ancient) Greek

In addition to the standard 24-letter Greek alphabet, the keyboard includes an extended set of polytonic characters needed for Classical and Byzantine Greek. These appear after the standard alphabet buttons and include:

  • Final Sigma (ς) – the form of sigma used at the end of a word.
  • Breathing marks: smooth (᾿) and rough (῾) – e.g. ἀ / ἁ, ἐ, ἰ, ὀ / ὁ, ὐ, ὡ.
  • Accent marks: acute (´), grave (`), and circumflex/perispomeni (͂) – applied to α, ε, η, ι, ο, υ, ω.
  • Iota subscript: ῃ (η with iota subscript), ῷ (ω with perispomeni and iota subscript).
  • Rho with rough breathing (ῥ / Ῥ).

Use the Shift button to switch between lowercase and uppercase forms of these extended characters where an uppercase counterpart exists.

The Shift Button

The Shift button toggles between lowercase and uppercase for languages that support both (Greek and Russian). The button label changes to Shift ON when uppercase mode is active. Clicking it again returns to lowercase. For Arabic, Farsi and Hebrew the Shift button is disabled because these scripts do not distinguish letter case.
